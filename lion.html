Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the most iconic and powerful big cats in the animal kingdom. Known as the "king of the jungle," lions are native to parts of Africa and a small population exists in the Gir Forest of India. They are the only cats that live in social groups called prides, which usually consist of related females, their cubs, and a small number of adult males.

Male lions are easily recognised by their majestic manes, which vary in colour and size and serve as a sign of maturity and strength. Female lions are the primary hunters in the pride, working together to take down prey such as wildebeest, zebras, and antelope.

Lions are carnivores and prefer open savannahs and grasslands where they can spot prey from a distance. They are also known for spending large portions of the day resting and conserving energy, often active only during early mornings and late evenings.
